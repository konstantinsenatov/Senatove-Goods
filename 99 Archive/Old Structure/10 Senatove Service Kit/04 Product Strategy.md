# Product Strategy

## Why WordPress-First
A WordPress-first product aligns with the intended user workflow: service businesses and studios need editable marketing pages without custom coding per page. WordPress provides a familiar CMS foundation, supports content operations, and allows curated control through a companion plugin while keeping premium frontend quality in theme.

## Why Not HTML/CSS-First
An HTML/CSS-only product would look polished but fail the operational goal: non-technical users need structured editability in admin, not static code handoffs. The product intent is long-term content management and repeatable page assembly, which requires CMS-native data handling, editor workflows, and update-safe architecture.

## Why Not WooCommerce-First
The first product is service-oriented, not catalog-first commerce. Prioritizing WooCommerce would shift scope to store complexity, checkout dependencies, and commerce UX constraints too early. Current strategic focus is premium service presentation, conversion-oriented page structure, and clean editorial storytelling.

## Target Audience
- Service-focused agencies and studios.
- Consultants and boutique firms with premium positioning.
- Productized service businesses needing elegant, controlled pages.
- Teams that want WordPress flexibility without a fully open-ended page builder.

## Intended User Workflow
1. Install and activate theme + plugin.
2. Open a page in WordPress admin.
3. Add curated sections from a visual library.
4. Fill structured fields per section.
5. Reorder and refine page composition.
6. Publish with premium frontend rendering handled by the theme.

## Desired Product Feel
- Premium and quiet visual confidence.
- Minimal and calm interface density.
- Warm editorial rhythm in typography and spacing.
- Controlled flexibility: enough customization for real projects, without layout chaos.

## Why This Is a Strong First Senatove Goods Product
- Clear differentiation: curated builder + premium theme pairing.
- Practical value: real admin usability, not just visual templates.
- Repeatable architecture for future vertical products.
- Balanced complexity for first release: ambitious but controllable.
